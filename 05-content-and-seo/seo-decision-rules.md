# WebReadyNow — SEO Decision Rules

**Purpose:** Load this file before making any SEO or content decision. No final SEO decision without the latest Growth Dashboard export.

---

## Required Inputs Before Any SEO Decision

1. Growth Dashboard Excel export (most recent)
2. Content Inventory (`content-inventory-summary.md`)
3. Offer Map (`04-offer-map.md`)
4. Safe Claims (`03-safe-claims.md`)
5. Relevant product or service file (if content is product/service-specific)
6. Case study proof if the content requires it (`08-proof/case-study-index.md`)

Do not make SEO decisions without these inputs. Ask for the Growth Dashboard export if it is not provided.

---

## Decision Framework

Every content decision must connect to all of these:

| Checkpoint | Question |
|---|---|
| Buyer pain | Is this a real problem a real buyer is searching for? |
| Search intent | Does the content match what the searcher actually wants? |
| Offer connection | Does this content connect to a WebReadyNow service or product? |
| Safe claims | Can WebReadyNow safely back every claim in this content? |
| Content inventory | Does this content already exist? If yes, should we update instead of create? |
| Internal linking | Where does this content link to and what links to it? |
| Conversion path | What is the CTA and what happens after the visitor clicks? |
| Social distribution | Can this be distributed on Facebook or other channels? |

---

## What NOT to Create

- Random informational content with no offer connection
- Content that implies capabilities the code or service does not support
- Service pages without defined delivery SOPs
- Content that duplicates existing published articles without a clear improvement reason
- Content targeting keywords with no realistic conversion path

---

## Content Priority Logic

Prioritize content in this order:

1. **Pain-connected service pages** — buyer is in pain, searching for help, has money to spend
2. **Case study content** — proof of delivery, supports trust and conversion
3. **Pain-based educational content** — attracts buyers who are investigating solutions
4. **Product documentation** — supports conversion and reduces support load
5. **Informational content** — lowest priority unless it connects strongly to a service

## Social Strategy

Facebook and social are part of the content distribution system.

Strong angles for social:
- Your checkout may be failing even when your site looks fine.
- A slow WooCommerce store is not always a hosting problem.
- Adding another plugin can create more risk than solving the real issue.
- Inventory sync problems usually start with unclear system ownership.
- Before rebuilding your WooCommerce store, audit the real problems first.

Every piece of content should have a potential social angle identified before publishing.

## SEO Action Classification

Use the lowest level that accurately captures the action. Do not update core knowledge base files for every small SEO action.

### Level 1 — Tactical Change
Single tactical change. One page. No new content. Title, meta, internal link, image alt, indexing check, or CTA tweak.

**Tracking required:** Action Log only.

### Level 2 — Page Optimization
Page optimization. Affects one page and its related cluster. Title, meta, content, and internal links may be updated. No new content created and no offer or strategy change.

**Tracking required:** Action Log + Content Inventory.

### Level 3 — Strategic Change
Strategic change. New page, new cluster, new offer, new content published, product/service positioning change, safe claim change, or multiple pages restructured.

**Tracking required:** Action Log + Content Inventory + relevant knowledge base core/product/service files + decision record if needed.
